# Impact Statement — Job Lander

**The problem.** A tailored résumé is the single biggest lever a candidate has against the hundreds of applicants per opening — but tailoring costs 30–60 minutes each: read the posting, find the experiences that matter, rewrite to mirror the role's language. Across the dozens of applications a real search demands, this forces an impossible trade-off: apply broadly with a generic résumé that gets filtered out, or apply selectively and miss opportunities. And that's after the daily grind of scanning multiple job boards just to *find* the right postings. The process is high-effort, low-yield, and demoralizing precisely when people can least afford it.

**Target audience.** Active job seekers — especially experienced professionals (PM, TPM, program/operations, strategy, and adjacent knowledge-work roles) who apply to many roles and know tailoring matters but can't sustain it by hand. Broadly, anyone whose search is bottlenecked by the time cost of finding *and* customizing.

**The solution & expected impact.** Job Lander is a dual-agent system that automates the search-and-tailor pipeline end to end while keeping the human in control. Agent 1 (Job Finder) aggregates fresh postings from LinkedIn, Indeed, and Glassdoor (via Google for Jobs), scores each against the seeker's résumé, and returns a ranked shortlist. After the seeker approves, Agent 2 (Resume Customizer) asks a short, consolidated set of clarifying questions, then tailors the résumé to each approved job and emails a ready-to-apply packet. Expected impact: **collapse hours of repetitive work into minutes**, and turn a scattershot search into a focused, high-quality application pipeline — more tailored applications submitted, submitted earlier, with less burnout.

**Why the AI solution is valuable.** The work is genuinely judgment-heavy, not just mechanical: reading a job description, deciding which real experiences to surface, and rephrasing them to fit — while never inventing anything. That is exactly where an LLM agent adds value over a rules-based automation. Job Lander pairs that judgment with three deliberate human-approval gates (which jobs to pursue, answering gaps with real facts, and the final decision to apply), so it amplifies the person instead of replacing their control. It also demonstrates a practical multi-agent pattern — specialized agents handing off to one another, gated by human approval, delivering a finished real-world outcome rather than just a chat response.
